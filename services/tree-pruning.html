Tree pruning

Tree pruning for stronger structure and healthier growth.

Selective pruning removes problem limbs, improves canopy balance, and helps trees grow in a healthier direction.

Pruning is detailed tree care, not just cutting.

Pruning focuses on the condition and structure of the tree. It can help reduce weak branches, crossing limbs, deadwood, and crowded growth that affects the tree over time.

For homes and properties in South County, pruning is especially helpful when trees grow near roofs, walls, sidewalks, driveways, or outdoor seating areas.

Pruning goals include:

  • Remove dead, weak, or crossing branches
  • Improve canopy structure
  • Reduce crowded or heavy growth
  • Protect roofs, fences, and walkways
  • Support healthier long-term growth
